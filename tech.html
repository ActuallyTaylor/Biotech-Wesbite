The Technology Behind Powering The Earth

Collecting Power

Power will be collected through arrays of solar panels dotted across the Moonâ€™s north and south poles. These solar panels will send their power to a central power station that will manage the sending of power to the satellite network surrounding Earth.

Sending & Receiving Power

At these stations, electricity will be converted into concentrated microwaves that will be aimed and shot towards a satellite orbiting Earth. This satellite will then store and beam its energy, using a concentrated microwave, back to ground stations on Earth. These ground stations will receive the microwaves and convert them back into usable electricity that will enter the power grid.

Keeping The Infrastructure Safe

To keep our solar infrastructure safe we will use liquid nitrogen to cool our Solar Panels while they are under direct sunlight. Our construction robots will do routine checks on the panels to make sure they have not been damaged by any micro-asteroids. If any panels are damaged, they will be replaced with spares. To protect the panels even more they will be covered by a protective layer made of plastic.

Distributing Energy

On Earth, ground stations will collect the microwaves and convert them back into electricity. This electricity will then be distributed out into the electrical grid for people to use.
